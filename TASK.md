# Coteach Task: Diagram Generation

> Source: https://tlvi.notion.site/Coteach-Task-Diagram-Generation-38f252dfaae080c199d0c4d4e7524999

## The Problem

One of the most common needs in K–12 lesson planning is the creation of math diagrams. Number lines, area models, and coordinate grids are often the centerpiece of instruction — yet they're surprisingly hard for language models to get right. Even frontier LLMs share the same failure modes: they make mathematical errors, lack visual consistency and fidelity, and miss the pedagogical purpose.

We want a system that turns a natural-language request into a classroom-ready image, ready to print and put in front of students. A request looks like this:

> Visual: A number line from 0 to 10 with every whole number labeled and evenly spaced tick marks. Purpose: Kindergarten students use the number line to count forward and locate whole numbers.

## What You're Provided

### A benchmark.

We've collected 30 requests that mirror real Coteach educator asks, spanning number lines, area models, bar graphs, and more. It's an authentic view of what K–12 educators actually ask for, but it's only a sample of the full stream.

### A naive solution.

We've built a miniature replica of our own diagram pipeline as a scaffold. It accepts a textual request and produces an image. While it works, it's intentionally naive — a single-sentence prompt asking a model to generate an SVG.

## Your Task

**Improve the diagram generator as much as you can in 3 hours.**

There's no single right answer — "correct" for these diagrams is genuinely open-ended, but some objective criteria exist, such as containing fewer mathematical errors, and avoiding visual artifacts. Working with LLMs requires more "taste" than ever, and this is an opportunity to demonstrate it.

How you achieve quality is entirely up to you. Your scope is unbounded — you're free to change the model, the image format, external APIs, and beyond. As long as your solution takes in a textual request and produces visual output, it's a valid solution. You're welcome to build a UI and tooling alongside your solution, but it's not required — we just want the core generator backend.

### Constraints

Expect that your solution will be used by real educators, which bounds what "better" means. The best-looking outputs might come from the largest models at max reasoning — but you pay for them in latency and cost, and a teacher won't wait. Even a perfectly correct diagram disappoints if it takes 60 seconds or costs a quarter to make. We aren't imposing hard constraints on cost or latency, but use your best judgement to weigh the overall user experience.

## Ground Rules

- **Timebox to 3 hours.** Stop where you'd naturally stop, and note what you'd do next.
- **Use AI tooling freely.** Work with whatever you'd normally reach for. We're interested in how you direct and verify it.

## How Solutions Are Evaluated

This is a hard problem. We've spent months on our own version, and we don't expect you to solve it in an afternoon. Instead, we're assessing how you ramp into a new problem space, explore the jagged frontier of what today's models can and can't do, and build intuition for working around their limitations to deliver real value to educators.

We'll be reviewing your code as well, but we are primarily interested in how you tackle an end-to-end problem, creatively evaluate trade-offs, and build something focused and complete under tight time constraints.
